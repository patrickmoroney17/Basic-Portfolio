About Me

My name is Patrick Moroney and I am a highly motivated and conscientious IT Project Manager with over 15 years’ experience, providing effective leadership and problem resolution, in both small (MNI) and large (Prudential, Optum / UHG) corporate environments. I have excellent communication skills and I can work under pressure efficiently to meet deadlines.

While my most recent job experiences have been project / application development management, in an effort to return to my technical coding roots, I recently completed Rutger’s Coding Bootcamp -- a 12-week Full Stack Flex course aimed at providing the necessary knowledge and skills to build dynamic end-to-end web applications and become a full stack web developer. The program was rigorous and fast-paced, covering both the theory and application of web development.

Working as a contractor this past year, I enjoyed successes at Prudential and CareCentrix. As a member Prudential’s PMO, I participated in a project to replace Prudential’s actuarial valuation system, defining data flows from the Annuity Administrative Systems -> Snapshot Data Lake -> ETL -> In-force Data Warehouse -> Calculation Engine -> Liability Warehouse -> FASB Compliant Reserves. At CareCentrix I worked as a core team member tasked with cutting over existing Oracle data warehouses to RAC for QA and production environments

Successes at Optum include delivering a replacement Affordable Care Act portal for the taxpayers of Massachusetts. Working both onsite and remote from Optum’s NJ Delivery Center, I worked closely with the software vendor, QA Leads, Release Management, infrastructure / operations partners, vendors, and business owners to drive process improvement, and add new features via continuous delivery over a 3-year period. Other work included participating in the design team of Optum’s proprietary Integrated Eligibility / ACA portal (data warehouse design, process flows, etc.), and management support for the Vermont HIX, West Virginia Operations and Maintenance engagements.

As Manager of Technology at MNI – a small newswire focused on fixed income and foreign exchange news & data, successes include overhauling their global news filing system, delivering a robust state of the art J2EE multi-datacenter solution feeding platforms like Bloomberg and Reuters. Another success was leading the development of an algorithmic data feed product for high frequency trading applications. The application was developed in house, and involved building out global telecommunication and server farm infrastructure in data centers in the US, Europe, and Asia. The result was one of the fastest products available.
